Japanese Table Manners

If you're at dinner party and receive drinks, you should wait to begin drinking.
After everyone is served, someone will take the lead to make a speech, and then raise his/her glass and yell, "Kampai!", meaning cheers.
You will receive a small wet cloth at most Japanese restaurants. Use this to wash your hands before eating.
Carefully fold and set it aside on the table. Do not use it as a napkin, or to touch a part of your face.
Slurping noodles or making loud noises while eating is OK!
In fact, slurping hot food like ramen is polite, to show you are enjoying it.
Before eating anything, it’s polite to say “itadakimasu" meaning "I will receive".
You should be saying this regardless if you are going to eat a seven-course meal or a sample at a supermarket.
You do not need to tip in Japan for cabs, restaurants, personal care, etc.
Tipping is actually insulting.
It is very disrespectful to eat food while walking in Japan.
Japanese culture dictacts that food deserves proper respect; i.e. sitting down to properly enjoy the taste and not just shoving it quickly in your face as you run errands.
If drinking, never refill your own glass as it signifies that your host is being unattentive.
Traditionally, drinks are poured by others but this works both ways. If someone’s drink is below half full, make sure to fill it for them!
Eat sushi with your hands. Place the the fish-side on your tongue and eat in one bite.
Only use chopsticks for sashimi and ginger. Ginger should be eaten between sushi pieces.
